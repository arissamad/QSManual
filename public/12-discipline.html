Discipline

The Disciplinary Incidents feature enables teachers and administrators to easily document and track student disciplinary issues. The incident report form enables teachers and administrators to include detailed information including student name, incident, response, demerit points, and action taken. The Disciplinary Incidents page is linked to the student, teacher, and response databases, so teachers and administrators can get much of an incident report complete with just a few clicks of the mouse. Information within the disciplinary incidents database can be sorted and filtered to find specific students and incidents quickly.

Responses are a way to organize incidents into groups for easy management. Use responses to help fairly distribute demerit points and to search for specific disciplinary issues. Responses are an interactive part of Incident reports, so faculty can categorize an incident quickly and have demerit points assigned to the student automatically.

This chapter provides detailed information on creating an incident report, managing the response database, and assigning demerit points. Features include adding, editing, sorting, and deleting incidents, and adding and managing responses and demerit points.

Introduction to Disciplinary Incident Reports

Disciplinary incident reports are a way for you to keep track of incidents in your school by student, incident type, incident details, and action taken. With integrated student, teacher, and responses databases, you can quickly fill out an incident report and save it to the disciplinary database so others can reference the incident immediately and the appropriate actions taken. An email to the parents can also be composed and sent at the time an incident report is written or edited.

Enabling and Disabling the Discipline Feature

By default the Discipline feature comes enabled. If your school does not need to use the Discipline feature, you may turn the feature off. The Discipline option is removed from the top menu bar giving you a customized look and feel. Likewise, if the Discipline option is not visible in the top menu bar, it is easily enabled for immediate use within your school.

  1. Click Features at the top of the page.

    The Features page opens.

  1. Go to the row that says Discipline.
  2. To disable the Discipline feature, uncheck the checkbox in the Discipline row. To enable the Discipline feature, check the checkbox in the Discipline row.

    3. The menu bar refreshes. If the Discipline feature is disabled, then the Discipline option will no longer be available in the top menu bar. If the Discipline feature is enabled, then the Discipline option will be available in the top menu bar.

  3. Click an item from the menu bar to continue. To configure the Discipline feature, see Setting General Discipline Preferences.

Accessing the Disciplinary Incidents Page

To access the Disciplinary Incidents page, click Discipline at the top of the page. If there is no Discipline option at the top of the page, see Enabling and Disabling the Discipline Feature.

The Incidents page opens. You are now ready to add your disciplinary incidents report.

Moving Around the Incidents Table

By default, 10 incidents are displayed on each page of the Incidents table. When more than 10 incidents are reported, a new page in the Incidents table is added. Use the navigation controls at the bottom of the Incidents table to scroll through the pages. To change the number of incidents shown on each page, see Setting Incident Table Preferences.

Adding an Incident

  1. Click Add Incident. The Add Incident form opens. This is where you will write your incident report.

  1. Click the Incident Date field. An interactive calendar opens.

  1. Use the calendar to select the date the incident occurred. There are two ways to change the date:
    • Click the arrowheads on either side of the month and year at the top of the calendar. Clicking the left arrow moves the months backwards. Clicking the right arrow moves the months forward. The years move backward or forwards when you reach January.
    • Click the arrow next to the month or year. A drop-down menu opens. Click the month or year you want from the drop-down menu. The month and year display on the calendar.
  2. Click the number of the day that the incident occurred. The calendar closes.
  3. Click the Student field. A drop-down list of students opens.
  4. Select the student that the incident report is about. The drop-down list closes.
  5. Click the Reported By field. A drop-down list of faculty opens.
  6. Select your name from the list. The drop-down list closes.
  7. Place your cursor in the Incident Detail text box. This is where you can describe the incident in detail.
  8. Type the details of the incident.
  9. In the Response field, click the down arrow. The Response drop-down menu opens.
  10. Select the appropriate response from the drop-down menu. The demerit field automatically displays the number of demerit points associated with the response. You may change the demerit point value by placing your cursor in the Demerit Points text box.

    11. Note: For more information about responses and demerit points, see Introduction to Responses and Demerit Points.

  11. Place your cursor in the Action Taken text box. This is where you can describe what the consequences, punishments, or repremands were as a result of the incident.
  12. Type what actions were taken because of the incident.
  13. Click Add to save your incident report to the database.
  14. You may choose to send an email to the parents or guardians of the student immediately. Under Notify Parents & Student via Email, check the email addresses to which you want to send your email.

  1. Place your cursor in the Email Subject field.
  2. Type the subject of your email.
  3. Place your cursor in the Email Message field.
  4. Type the text of your email message.
  5. When you are finished, click Send Email & Save Incident. Your email is sent to the selected email addresses and the incident saved to the database.

Editing an Incident

  1. From the Incidents table, click on the incident you want to edit. The Edit Incident form opens.

  1. Edit the incident report as necessary.
  2. Click Add to save your edited incident report to the database.
  3. If you want to send an email to the parents or guardians of the student, select the email recipients and type the subject and text of your email message.
  4. When you are finished, click Send Email & Save Incident. Your email is sent to the selected email addresses and the incident saved to the database.

    5. For more information about completing an incident report, see Adding an Incident.

Searching for an Incident

The search feature enables you to search for specific incidents by student name, incident date, or both.

  1. Place your cursor in the Search field.

  1. Type in the name or word for which you are searching.
  2. You can begin your search two ways:
    • Press Enter on your keyboard.
    • Click the magnifying glass icon at the end of the search field.

    The results of your search display in the Incidents table.

  1. To return to the main Incidents table, click Clear Search below the Search field.

Using the Search Form

Use the Search form to quickly find student names and incident dates. You can search by name, date, or by both name and date.

  1. Click the arrowhead at the end of the Search field. The Search form opens.

  1. If you know the student's name, place your cursor in the Student Name field.
  2. Enter the student's first name, last name, or both.
  3. If you know the date of the incident, click the Incident Date field. An interactive calendar opens.

  1. Use the calendar to select the incident date. There are two ways to change the date:
    • Click the arrowheads on either side of the month and year at the top of the calendar. Clicking the left arrow moves the months backwards. Clicking the right arrow moves the months forward. The years move backward or forwards when you reach January.
    • Click the arrow next to the month or year. A drop-down menu opens. Click the month or year you want from the drop-down menu. The month and year display on the calendar.
  2. Click the number of the day that the incident occurred. The calendar closes.
  3. To search a range of dates, click the second Incident Date field. The interactive calendar opens.
  4. Select the date you want to search to. The calendar closes.
  5. To search only for incidents that have been deleted, check Show Only Deleted Items.

  1. Click Search to start the search. The results of your search display in the Incidents table.

    2. Note: To cancel your search, click Cancel.

  2. To return to the main Incidents table, click Clear Search below the Search field.

Deleting an Incident

Incidents can be deleted from the Incidents table when they are no longer relevant or needed. Incidents that are deleted from the table are not deleted from the Incidents database and can be viewed at any time or undeleted. See Viewing a Deleted Incident and Undeleting an Incident. You can delete incidents from the main Incidents table or from the Edit Incidents form.

Incidents can be deleted two ways: Directly from the Incidents table and from the Edit Incidents form.

    Deleting an Incident from the Incidents Table

  1. In the first column of the table, click the checkbox of the incident you want to delete.
  2. Click the checkbox in the column header. A pop-up menu opens.

  1. Select one of the three options:
    AllDeletes all items in the Incidents table whether checked or not
    NoneDoes not delete any incidents from the Incidents table and clears all checkmarks
    Delete SelectedOnly deletes items that are checked in the Incidents table
  2. When the confirmation dialog box opens, click Yes, Delete to delete the incident or Cancel.

    Deleting an Incident Using the Edit Incidents Form

  1. From the Incidents table, click on the incident you want to delete. The Edit Incident form opens.
  2. Click Click here. The incident is deleted.

Setting General Discipline Preferences

Disciplinary preferences give you control over writing, viewing, and running student disciplinary records. You probably will want some faculty members to have total access to these records, some with only partial access, and maybe even some faculty members with no access to student disiplinary records at all. Disciplinary preferences enable you to define who can view student disciplinary records, who can write disciplinary reports, who can run disciplinary reports, and if parents can view their child's reports.

  1. From the top of the page, click Features.

    The Features page opens.

  1. Go to the row that says Discipline.

  1. Click Configure. The Discipline configuration dialog box opens.

  1. Set the following options:
    • All teachers can enter and view their own reports-Check this option to enable all faculty members to write disciplinary incident reports and view the reports they have written. This option does not allow faculty members to view reports written by others.
    • Select users who can globally manage disciplinary incidents-Add faculty members from the interactive list who can write, edit, view, and delete all incident reports.
    • Teachers can view all incidents, even those written by other teachers-Check this option to enable all faculty members to view all disciplinary reports regardless of who wrote them.
    • Parents can view incidents for their children-Check this option to enable parents to view all incident reports for their children.
    • Who can run discipline reports?-Add faculty members from the interactive list who can run disciplinary reports.
  2. Click Save to save your changes. Click Cancel to go back to the Features page without saving your changes.

Setting Incident Table Preferences

You can set how many rows you want the Incidents table to display on each page. By default, 10 rows are displayed on each page.

  1. Click the preferences icon at the bottom left of the Incidents table. A pop-up menu opens.

  1. Select the number of rows you want to display on each page from the pop-up menu. The table automatically updates.
  2. To set a number of rows that is different than the preset options, select Custom. The Number of Rows to Display dialog box opens.
  3. In the text field, type the number of rows you want to display. The number must be between 1 and 1000.
  4. Click OK to set the number of rows or Cancel to leave the number of rows unchanged.

Introduction to Responses and Demerit Points

Responses are a way to group, or categorize, like incidents. Responses are not incident details or actions taken as a result of an incident, but are a general grouping of similar events for the purpose of assigning demerit points and running searches. For example, name calling, teasing, and insults might all be grouped together under a specific response called 'taunting' or a more general group called 'minor.' Responses can be customized for your school's needs and can be very general, like severe, major, moderate, and minor, or very specific, like truancy, fighting, class disruption, or forgetting homework. You can make as few or as many different response types as you want.

When the 'taunting' response was added to the Responses database, it was assigned a demerit point value of 10. The responses database is linked to your Incident reports, so when a faculty member selects 'taunting' as a response to an incident, then 10 demerit points are automatically added to the student's record. The responses database takes the guesswork out of assigning demerit points to students creating a truly fair and impartial system.
