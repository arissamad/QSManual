Introduction

Welcome to the online manual for QuickSchools, your school management system for K-12 schools! Here you will find everything you need to know to understand and navigate all the features QuickSchools has to offer students, parents, teachers and administrators. QuickSchools puts all aspects of school-related information at your fingertips, from admissions to academics, including attendance, billing and fees, extra-curricular activities, grades, transcripts, and report cards for individual students. It is a quick and easy way to facilitate record-keeping and improve communication between families, teachers, and administrators in the school, enabling parents to feel more connected and be better informed about the educational experience their students are receiving.

For teachers and administrators, QuickSchools streamlines the record-keeping process, providing consistent, school-wide management of grading, attendance, fee tracking, and disciplinary action, so that less time can be spent on mundane aspects of administrative record-keeping and data collection, and more on putting that information to good use to achieve excellence in education. Students can also use QuickSchools to track their educational progress and improve performance, either in a single course or throughout their entire school experience. Using QuickSchools, it is easy to see at a glance how students are doing, what homework is due, and to get feedback directly from teachers on tests, quizzes, and assignments.

This manual is a work-in-progress and we greatly appreciate your input and discussion of what you find presented here. Eventually, filters on the left-hand side of the page will enable you to customize the manual for your own use as either a student, parent, teacher or administrator. Our vision is that the online manual is an evolving document, so there will be opportunities for visitors to make comments and provide input on each section as you utilize it. In the meantime, feel free to send your feedback to support@quickschools.com

Logging Into QuickSchools for the First Time

When you initially set up a QuickSchools account for your school, you are asked to provide a name or abbreviation for your school that will be used as part of the URL for your own unique QuickSchools site. This unique web address is where you will go to log into the QuickSchools site for your school.

Once you have reached your QuickSchools site using your unique URL, you may login using the username and password you set up when you registered your account.

Using the Homepage

Logging in will bring you to your personal home page, displaying the "Teacher's Lounge," as well as a boxed "To Do List," and an "Events List." This page functions as a bulletin board where messages may be posted and read, and on which you can keep track of tasks and scheduled events.

Teacher's Lounge

The Teacher's Lounge is the first thing you will see when you log in to your QuickSchools account. Teachers and administrators can leave messages or comments here, which can be seen by everyone with a teacher or administrator account. This makes it easy to distribute announcements or discuss policy issues without having to coordinate schedules for a physical meeting of personnel.

Posting a message is as easy as typing into the box at the top of the page and clicking on the Post button (). This will cause it to appear as a new entry at the top of the Teacher's Lounge. A file or image can be added by clicking on the "Add photo/file " link, and selecting one from the hard drive.

You can also easily insert a comment, or add a file or image to an existing comment, by clicking on the relevant link directly under the comment to which you want to address, as shown above. A comment box will appear and you can type in your entry and then click on the Post button () to add it to the Teacher's Lounge.

To Do List

The To Do List is another convenient feature available on the home page after login. It consists of a small dialog box, and functions as a reminder of things you need to accomplish.

To add an item to your To Do List, click on the "+" icon () at the top right corner of the box. This will automatically insert a line into your box, which you can then overwrite with your desired text to create your list item, as shown above. Note that you can easily change the order of items listed in your To Do List by simply clicking on them and dragging them to the desired location inside the box.

You can check items off your To Do List as you would on any paper checklist, by simply clicking on the box to the left of the item.

This results in the addition of a strikethrough line, effectively crossing out the item. You may choose to leave the completed item in your To Do List, or you may remove it by clicking on the "Clear Completed" link which appears at the top of the box after an item is checked as completed.

Events List

The Events List is a great way to keep track of key events occurring in the school. You can add events to your list by clicking on the "+" icon () at the top right of the box, and the filter feature lets you sort and display all, upcoming, or past events.

When you click on the "+" icon (), an Add Event popup box will appear, in which you are asked to enter aspects of your event: the date, the name of the event, as well as with whom you wish to share the event (only yourself; all teachers; teachers and parents; or teachers, parents, and students). Anyone you have selected to share the event will automatically find it in their own Events box on login to QuickSchools.

When you have filled out the information, click on the red Add button () at the bottom of the box to add the event to your own Events List and the lists of those with whom you have indicated will share the item.

Setting Up Your Profile

There are two ways to access and edit your QuickSchools profile: you can click on the My Profile link in the dark blue border at the top of the page, or you can click directly on your user portrait (the image in the top right-hand corner) to get to your profile.

Either method will bring you to your profile page, which looks like this:

Although the username you use to log in is not editable, you may change your full name, preferred name, phone contact, and email address on the My Profile page. You can also upload an image to associate with your username by clicking on the Upload Photo button or dragging and dropping an image onto the Profile Picture box.

Don't forget to click on the red Save button () to finalize your profile changes.

You may also opt to change your password you use to login to your QuickSchools account on the My Profile page. Just enter the old password, and then the new password (and then a second time to confirm), and click on the red Change Password button () to make your change go into effect.

Activating Features

If you are a school administrator with privileges for activating features and designating who in the school may access different features in various roles, you will see the Features link in the blue border at the top of your page. There are several different features settings that may be changed using this tab, as explained below.

Accessing Features

Clicking on this link will display the features settings that are available to your school account. There are currently three different plans available, each of which include different features: the Gaia, Apollo, and Athena plans.

Changing Plans

The plan in which your QuickSchools account is currently enrolled will be clearly marked by colored highlighting and the words "You're on the ____ Plan" at the top of the highlighted column (the Apollo Plan is shown as the enrolled one in the above example). You can easily upgrade or downgrade your plan by mousing over one of the other two choices and clicking on it. The new plan will be highlighted in a different color when you mouse over it (here we have shown the Athena plan highlighted in red as an example).

Clicking on the new plan choice will bring up a confirmation popup box:

To change plans, click on the red Change button () to confirm your selection.

Selecting and Configuring Features

To turn on features within your plan, click the checkbox corresponding to the feature you want toggled on, and make sure that there is a check in the box indicating that the feature is active.

Additional configuration of features is possible by clicking on the Configure link to the right of each checkbox, which will bring up customizable configuration options for the specific feature selected. The content of these configurations varies from feature to feature, but often includes setting access privileges for the feature - who may have access to that feature, and what the limits of their access will be. These settings are explained in detail in the relevant sections for the given features in subsequent parts of this manual.

Configuring General Settings

At the top right of the Features page, you will see an Edit Overall Settings button (). Clicking on this will open a series of menus through which you can make general settings selections for your school's account. These include U.S. or U.K.-style configuration, default nationality, selection of personnel with access to new semester setup, enabling college-level settings, homeroom name setup, subject enrollment with homeroom grouping, advanced control of backup options, and whether or not to display the Teacher's Lounge and the Getting Started page.

As always, don't forget to click on the red Save button () to finalize your overall settings changes before leaving this screen.

Managing Terms and Semesters

Quickschools New Semester Setup enables you to start a new semester or school year from existing QuickSchools databases and information. With the New Semester Setup feature, QuickSchools can automatically promote all current students to the next grade level. This feature not only enables you to promote the entire school in a single click, but all of the students’ historical information, like attendance, grades, billing, and profile information, moves with them.

This same feature can be used mid-year as well when the winter semester ends and the spring semester begins and you want to start a new grading period. Simply create a new semester without promoting the students up a grade. All student schedules and related information is saved.

This feature provides the flexibility and customization you need to accommodate your school's specific grading periods and acadmic year. Grading periods can set up yearly or by semesters or quarters. So that you only have to set up your school's term information once, create and save templates with your semester information, year information, grade promotion structure, homeroom teachers, and start and end dates and apply them easily and quickly to the entire student body year after year.

Changing a Term

Changing semesters and school years is easy: Click New Semester Setup at the top of the page.

From the New Semester window, select what you want to do.

Creating a New Semester

From the Time to Setup a New Semester window, click Create New Semester. The Create a New Semester Within the Current Year window opens. This option enables you to start a new semester or grading period without promoting students to the next grade.

You can select a saved template for your new semester, which provides the semester name and start and end dates, or create your own semester by selecting Create New Semester from the Target Semester drop-down menu and entering a semester name and start and end dates. Click Create New Semester when you are finished.

For information about creating semester templates, see Semester Management.

Creating a New Year

From the Time to Setup a New Semester window, click Create New Semester and Year. The Create a New Semester for the Next Academic Year window opens. This option enables you to start a new semester or grading period and promotes students to the next grade.

You can select a saved template for your new semester, which provides the semester name and start and end dates, or create your own semester by entering a new year and semester names in the New Year Name and New Semester Name fields and start and end dates in the Start and End Dates fields.

Grade promotions and homeroom teachers can also be defined while setting up your new year. Use the drop-down menus to make the appropriate selections. Click Create New Semester when you are finished.

For information about creating semester templates, see Semester Management.

Semester Management

Semester management is where you can create and manage semester templates which can be used when setting up new semesters or new school years. To view the Semester Management window, click View All Semesters from the Time to Setup a NEw Semester window. The Semester Management window opens.

Note: Semester management is for creating and maintaining semester templates. It does not begin or end semesters or grading periods.
