Attendance

The Attendance tab makes it easy for teachers, administrators, parents, and students to track attendance in three different ways at the school: by Daily Attendance, by Period-Based Attendance, or by Subject-Based Attendance. Teachers will be able to use the QuickSchools Attendance tab to quickly and efficiently take attendance, and attendance data can be easily rolled into a wide variety of different attendance reports, which can track attendance by homeroom class, by grade, by period, or by day using Excel spreadsheets.

Activating and Configuring Attendance Feature and Privileges

Before getting started with the Attendance tab, it is necessary to make sure that the Attendance feature is configured for your QuickSchools account, and to confirm who in your school will have the administrative privileges necessary to take attendance, configure attendance records, and create attendance reports. You will need to first manage your plan through the Features link to make sure that the Attendance feature is turned on and that the correct users have the access they need to work with attendance rosters and reports.

First, find the Features link in the top header of your QuickSchools screen and click on it. This will bring up your school's plan and account page with available features. All three of the QuickSchool plans (Gaia, Apollo, and Athena) allow access to the Attendance feature.

Make sure that there is a check in the Attendance row under your plan. This toggles the feature on so you can work on Attendance. Then click on the Configure link next to the box to set up Attendance privileges for your school:

This will bring up an Attendance popup box in which privileges can be set for personnel in your school. You can designate who in your school may take attendance, who can run attendance reports, and whether or not your school will take attendance more than once a day. For period-based attendance, you can also designate which teachers can configure the periods and subjects involved, as shown below:

After you have added the personnel you want to have access to these tools, scroll to the bottom of the popup box and click on the red Save button () to register your changes in the system. Now that you have configured the Attendance feature for your school, you are ready to begin taking attendance!

Accessing Attendance Tab

To access the Attendance section of QuickSchools from your login page, click on the Attendance link in the gray bar at the top of the screen, as shown:

This will bring up the main Report Cards page, on which you will see three different menu tabs:

  • Homeroom Attendance
  • Subject Attendance
  • Attendance Settings

The default tab is Homeroom Attendance, which loads the page you will need to take attendance in the homeroom.

Homeroom Attendance

The first time you enter this screen, only the date and the red Take Attendance button () will be displayed; you must click on it in order to load the list of students for the homeroom class you are working on.

To take homeroom attendance, first make sure you are on the Homeroom Attendance page by clicking on the correct tab at the top of the screen. Next, check to see that you have selected the correct teacher name from the drop-down Homeroom: menu on the right-hand side. Note that unless you have privileges to take another teacher's homeroom attendance, your selection will be limited to your own name.

Finally, confirm that the date displayed is the one for which you would like to record attendance. The current date will be the default but you can scroll backwards day by day using the arrow to the left of the displayed date.

The students enrolled in the designated homeroom you have selected will now be displayed, listed by default in a table of ten names per screen. This display setting can be easily modified to show more students on a single page, as described below:

Modifying Page Display to Change Number of Rows Per Page

The number of records displayed on the screen may be set and/or customized by clicking on this icon () in the very last row in the table. This reveals the following selection menu:

As indicated, the default setting is to display 10 rows on the screen; additional rows will then be shown on subsequent screens. You may change this to enable viewing of 25, 50, or 100 column categories at a time. The number of rows can also be customized to select all records by selecting the Custom selection, in which case you will be prompted to fill in the desired number of rows to be made visible per page by the following popup box:

Enter the desired number of rows to display and click on the Ok button () to save your preference.

Finally, to restore the selection to the default, select Clear Preferences from the selection menu. This will erase any prior selection you may have made and revert to the default view of the list of records on the Homeroom Attendance tab.

Taking Homeroom Attendance

As you can see, students for the designated teacher's homeroom are listed alphabetically in the attendance table with categories Present, Absent, Tardy, Tardy with Excuse, Absent with Excuse set up as options marked by a radio button selection, as shown:

The default setting for all students is Present. To mark a student absent or tardy, and either excused or unexcused, click on the appropriate button under the corresponding column heading for that student.

Additional menu items can be displayed for each student in the class by placing the cursor on the row corresponding to that student. This enables links called Details, Remove, and Add Remark to become available, as described below.

Details About Individual Student Attendance Record

Clicking on the Details link in a row next to a student's name (remember to mouseover the row to make this link visible) brings up a color-coded popup box displaying the student's attendance record for the month:

The color-coded popup makes it easy to see at a glance to what exent the student has been present, absent, and tardy in a given month, and whether tardiness and absences were excused or unexcused. Note that the pie chart give statistics for the student's attendance record for the whole term, even though only one month of actual attendance is displayed in the calendar. The total number of days of attendance recorded is indicated at the top of the box.

To change the month displayed, click on the arrow next to the month shown. An individual day of homeroom attendance can be reviewed by placing your cursor on the relevant calendar square, which causes an informational popup box to appear. This box includes the attendance category saved, the name of the homeroom teacher, and the name of the teacher or administrator who recorded the attendance on that day. To edit the attendance for that day, click on the calendar dates and a new Edit Attendance popup will appear:

Attendance status for that day can now be changed by selecting a new status from the drop-down menu. A remark to indicate the reason for the change may also be added in the Remark field. Then click the red Update button () to save the changes and return to the student detail box.

To exit the detail box and return to the main Homeroom Attendance page, click the red OK button () at the bottom of the popup.

Removing Student from Attendance Record

A student can be removed from the attendance list for a single day, or for multiple days over a period of time, which can be useful if you know a student will not be in school for an extended period.

Clicking on the Remove link in a row next to a student's name (remember to mouseover the row to make this link visible) brings up a Confirm Removal popup box that looks like this:

Click on the red Confirm Removal button () to confirm that you wish to remove the student from the current day's attendance record. If you would like to remove the student from more than one day of attendance records, click on the link provided. This will bring up a different Confirm Removal popup box allowing you to select removal dates, as shown:

Choose your "From" and "To" dates and then click on the red Confirm Removal button () to confirm that you wish to remove the student from the attendance record for those days.

Adding Remark to Student Attendance Record

Clicking on the Add Remark link in a row under the Remark column next to a student's name (remember to mouseover the row to make this link visible) enables you to type a remark into the box in the Remark column. The text will automatically be saved after you enter it.

Clear Attendance/Refresh Class List

To reset aspects of the Homeroom Attendance page, scroll to the bottom right corner and you will see two links: Clear Attendance and Refresh Class List:

Clear Attendance can be used in the event that attendance for a given day was erroneously taken and needs to be reset. Refresh Class List will update the list of students in the homeroom attendance, and can be used to include students recently enrolled in the class who are not yet appearing on the attendance roster.

Attendance Settings

On the Attendance Settings page, you can configure the type of attendance for each grade in the school; it can be taken daily, by period, or by subject. Note that daily attendance is also called homeroom attendance, and period attendance is attendance taken multiple times per day in the homeroom class.

Setting Daily Attendance

Selecting Daily Attendance for a grade will leave the default setting in place, enabling the homeroom teacher for that class to take attendance once per day through the Homeroom Attendance tab.

Setting Period-Based Attendance

Selecting Period-Based Attendance for a grade will open a series of boxes in the settings grid, so that you can designate how many times per day attendance should be taken by the homeroom teacher.

You must enter the number "1" or a greater in the box for each day, regardless of whether or not it is a school day. Attempting to enter a value of zero or leaving a box blank will result in the following error message:

For weekend days and other non-school days, put "1" in the box and simply do not take attendance. Entering a number greater than one will result in the appearance of multiple period tabs on the Homeroom Attendance page, corresponding to the number of periods entered for that day. In the above example, the settings indicate that attendance will be taken three times daily on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, but just once a day on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

Taking attendance on Friday, then, results in the appearance of three period tabs on the Homeroom Attendance page, as shown:

This is useful when you wish to document partial day absences, or track attendance specifically in one period of the day.

Setting Subject-Based Attendance

Selecting Subject-Based Attendance for a grade will open a series of boxes in the settings grid (similar to what you see for Period-Based Attendance), so that you can designate the subjects in which attendance will be taken, and also designate in which periods (or how many times per day) attendance should be taken.

You must enter the number "1" or a greater in the box for each day, regardless of whether or not it is a school day. Attempting to enter a value of zero or leaving a box blank will result in the following error message:

For weekend days and other non-school days, put "1" in the box and simply do not take attendance. Entering a number greater than one will result in the appearance of multiple period tabs on the Subject-Based Attendance page, corresponding to the number of periods entered for that day. In the above example, the settings indicate that attendance will be taken once daily on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Friday, but twice a day on Thursdays.

To configure Subject-Based Attendance, you must click on the Configure link on the right-hand side of the Grade column. This will open a popup box containing a grid corresponding to the days of the week and number of periods selected per day, as shown:

Now you can click to configure each school day and period by designating which subjects are being taught, and when attendance should be taken. Note that multiple subjects can be taught by different teachers in the same period (as shown on Thursday, Period 1), or a subject might extend over multiple periods during which attendance could be taken more than once (as shown in the case of History, offered over periods 1-2 on Thursdays).

The Click to configure link brings up a popup box with a list of teachers and courses offered for that grade, which then can be designated as subjects in which to take attendance. Placing courses in the box will result in the generation of an attendance sheet for that subject in the Subject Attendance tab.

Click the red Save button ()to complete the configuration process and return to the subject time table. Each subject listed in the grid will now have its own corresponding attendance record, accessible through the Subject Attendance tab, as described above in the Taking Subject Attendance section of this chapter of the manual.
