Parent Messaging

Parent Messaging is a powerful communications feature that enables schools to send messages to groups of parents or the entire school at one time through email, text messages, or voice messages. Create email and voice message templates for frequently sent messages including school closures, field trip information, or tuition information. You can also create and save recipient lists for groups that are contacted frequently such as the PTA, the school committee, or specific grade levels.

This chapter provides detailed information on creating emails, text messages, and voice messages for mass mailing.

Introduction to Parent Messaging

Parent Messaging is a powerful, highly customizable tool for communicating with parents and students quickly. The QuickSchools email, text messaging, and voice messaging features are integrated with the student database, making student and parent names, email addresses, and phone numbers available with a click of the mouse. Create groups of frequently contacted people, like the PTA, the debate team, or grade-specific students to quickly and easily add large numbers of recipients to your messages. To further streamline mass communications, save templates of your most frequently used communications so you do not have to repeatedly recreate the same messages.

Enabling and Disabling Parent Messaging

By default the Parent Messaging feature comes enabled. If your school does not need to use the Parent Messaging feature, you may turn the feature off. The Parent Messaging option is removed from the top menu bar giving you a customized look and feel. Likewise, if the Parent Messaging option is not visible in the top menu bar, it is easily enabled for immediate use within your school.

Click Features at the top of the page to open the Features page.

The Features page opens. From the Features page, you can enable or disable Parent Messaging.

When the Parent Messaging feature is enabled or disabled, the menu bar on the top of the page refreshes. If the Parent Messaging feature is disabled, then the Parent Messaging option will no longer be available in the top menu bar. If the Parent Messaging feature is enabled, then the Parent Messaging option will be available in the top menu bar.

Accessing the Parent Messaging Page

To access the Parent Messaging page, click Parent Messaging at the top of the page. If there is no Parent Messaging option at the top of the page, see Enabling and Disabling Parent Messaging.

The Send Email page opens. You are now ready to create and send messages.

Setting Parent Messaging Preferences

Parent Messaging preferences enable you to select who can send messages, who can run reports, and set the default country code for phone numbers.

Click Features at the top of the page to open the Features page.

The Features page opens. From the Parent Messaging row, click Configure.

Set your desired preferences from the Preferences dialog box. Select who can send messages and run reports by clicking in the text boxes to access a drop-down menu of faculty member names.

Click Save to save your changes or Cancel to leave your preferences unchanged.

Sending Emails

With the Parent Messaging feature, you can compose and send emails right from QuickSchools. In addition, you can make templates for emails that are sent frequently so that you don't have to keep retyping them. QuickSchools keeps a log of all sent email messages so you can quickly reference what emails have been sent and to whom.

Composing and Sending an Email

From the Send Email page, you can compose your email. First, make sure your name is displayed at the top of the form. If your name is incorrect, click Change. Enter your name and email address in the appropriate text boxes. Next, select the names of the students whose parents you want to email by clicking the To the Parents of text box and selecting students from the drop-down menu. Check Important Message-Send even if the parent opted not to receive emails if the email is important and you want every parent in your "send to" list to receive your email even if it overrides their preference not to receive emails from the school. If you want to send the email to teachers or students, click Teachers or Students and select their names from the drop-down menus. Next, in the Subject line, type the subject of your email. If you want to add an attachment, such as a file, form, or picture, click Attach a File. Finally, write the text of your email. Click Send Email to send the email or Clear to erase the message.

Creating, Editing, and Deleting an Email Template

You can save your email message as a template so that you can send the message again without retyping the email. After composing your email, click Save As Template. The email is saved and the new template displays in the Email Templates table.

To edit a template, go to the Email Template table, find the template you want to edit, and click Edit Template. Make any necessary changes, then click Save.

To delete an email template, go to the Email Template table, find the template you want to delete, and click Edit Template. Click Delete Template from the Edit Template dialog box. A dialog box displays asking you to confirm the deletion. Click Delete to delete the template or Cancel to leave the template unchanged.

Using an Email Template

Once an email template is created, it is easy to resend frequently used email messages. Go to the Email Template table, find the template you want to use, and click Use Template. The send email form is filled in with the information from the template. You can make any edits or changes to the email. Add recipients and attachments to the email as necessary. Click Send Email to send the email.

Searching for an Email Template

The search feature enables you to search for a specific email template by subject or by words or phrases within the email message. Place your cursor in the Search field and type in the word or phrase for which you are searching. Begin your search by pressing Enter on your keyboard or by clicking the magnifying glass icon at the end of the search field. The results of your search display in the Email Template table. To clear the search results, click Clear Search below the Search field.

Viewing Email History and Details

All emails sent through QuickSchools are recorded in the Email History table so that you can quickly find and refer to past email messages. Messages are sorted by date. The Status column shows how many emails were successfully sent, how many emails could not be sent, and any error messages. Some email messages and recipient lists are too long to display in the table, so a summary is shown. You can view the complete email and recipient list of a sent email message by clicking View Details or Show Full Message. The Email History table is located below the Send Email form.

Sorting the Email Templates Table

You can sort the Email Template table in ascending or descending order by clicking the arrow icon at the top of the table column. Email templates are sorted by subject line. An arrowhead pointing up indicates the table is sorted in ascending order. An arrowhead pointing down indicates the table is sorted in descending order.

Setting Email Table Preferences

You can set how many rows you want the Email History and Email Templates tables to display on each page. By default, 10 rows are displayed in each table. Click the preferences icon at the bottom, left of the tables. A pop-up menu opens. Select the number of rows you want to display on each page from the pop-up menu. The table automatically updates. To set a number of rows that is different than the preset options, select Custom. The Number of Rows to Display dialog box opens. In the text field, type the number of rows you want to display. The number must be between 1 and 1000. Click OK to set the number of rows or Cancel to leave the number of rows unchanged.

Sending Text Messages

The QuickSchools Text Message feature enables you to send text messages to parents, teachers, and students. QuickSchools keeps a log of sent text messages so you can reference what text messages have been sent.

There is a small fee charged per recipient when sending a text message.

Composing and Sending a Text Message

To access the text message feature, click Text Message.

From the Text Message page, you can write your text message. Select the names of the students whose parents you want to text message by clicking the To the Parents of text box and selecting students from the drop-down menu. Check Important Message-Send even if the parent opted not to receive text messages if the text message is important and you want every parent in your "send to" list to receive your text message even if it overrides their preference not to receive text messages from the school. If you want to send the text message to teachers or students, click Teachers or Students and select their names from the drop-down menus. Finally, write your text message. Click Send Text Message to send the message or Clear to erase the message.

Viewing Text Message History

All Text Messages sent through QuickSchools are recorded in the Text Message History table so that you can quickly find and refer to past text messages. Messages are sorted by date. The Status column shows how many text messages were successfully sent, how many text messages could not be sent, and any error messages. The Text Message History table is located below the text message form.

Setting Text Message History Table Preferences

You can set how many rows you want the Text Message History table to display on each page. By default, 10 rows are displayed. Click the preferences icon at the bottom, left of the table. A pop-up menu opens. Select the number of rows you want to display on each page from the pop-up menu. The table automatically updates. To set a number of rows that is different than the preset options, select Custom. The Number of Rows to Display dialog box opens. In the text field, type the number of rows you want to display. The number must be between 1 and 1000. Click OK to set the number of rows or Cancel to leave the number of rows unchanged.

Sending Voice Messages

Voice messaging is another powerful communications tool within Parent Messaging. With QuickSchools Voice Messaging, you can record and save your most frequently sent voice messages, send your messages to multiple people in one click, and track your past voice messages.

There is a small fee charged per recipient when sending a voice message.

Recording, Editing, and Deleting a Voice Message

Before you can send a voice message, you need to record it. Recording messages is done right from your own phone. Simply enter your phone number and QuickSchools calls you. After you record your message, it is saved in the Pre-Recorded Voice Messages table and is ready to be sent.

To record your voice message, go to the Voice Message page.

Click Record a Message Now. The Record New Message dialog box opens.

Enter the name of your message as you would like it to appear in the Pre-Recorded Voice Messages table. Next, starting with the area code, enter the phone number of the phone you would like to record from. The default country code is automatically displayed, but can be edited if necessary. Click Call Me Now and QuickSchools calls your phone. When you answer, follow the instructions given to record your voice message. Hang up when you are done. It is that easy.

When you finish recording your message, QuickSchools transcribes and posts your message to the Pre-Recorded Voice Messages table. Click Play to listen to your message. You can make any edits or changes to your message or delete your message by clicking Edit Details.

Sending a Voice Message

Now that you have recorded your message, you can address and send it quickly and easily. From the Send Voice Message page, select which phone message you want to send from the Pre-Recorded Voice Message table on the right. Click Use this Message to select it. The name of the Voice Message displays at the top of the Voice Message form. Next, select the names of the students whose parents you want to send the voice message to by clicking the To the Parents of text box and selecting students from the drop-down menu. If you want to send the email to teachers or students, click Teachers or Students and select their names from the drop-down menus. From the Which Phone to Call drop-down menu, select which phone option you prefer. Click Call Phones when you are ready to send the voice message.

Setting a Call Back Number

Parents may try to call the school back after receiving a voice message. You can set the phone number you would like parents to reach when they call back. For example, you may want to direct parent's return calls to a specific person in the office or to the school's answering machine so that the front desk is not overwhelmed with phone calls about the voice message.

To set a call back number, go to the Voice Message page. The current call back number is displayed under the Send Voice Message heading. To change the call back number, click Change.

The Enter School Phone Number for Call Backs dialog box opens. Enter the area code and phone number you would like parents to reach when they return your voice message call. If you are entering an international phone number, enter the country code. Click OK to change the number or Cancel to leave the number unchanged.

Searching for a Pre-Recorded Voice Message

The search feature enables you to search for a specific voice message by subject or by words or phrases within the transcribed voice message. Place your cursor in the Search field and type in the word or phrase for which you are searching. Begin your search by pressing Enter on your keyboard or by clicking the magnifying glass icon at the end of the search field. The results of your search display in the Pre-Recorded Voice Messages table. To clear the search results, click Clear Search below the Search field.

Viewing Call History and Details

All voice messages sent through QuickSchools are recorded in the Call History table so that you can easily find past voice messages. Messages are sorted by date and listed by voice message name. The Status column shows how many voice messages were successfully sent, how many voice messages could not be sent, and any error messages. To view the message and its recipient list, click View Details.

Sorting the Pre-Recorded Voice Messages Table

You can sort the Pre-Recorded Voice Messages Template table in ascending or descending order by clicking the arrow icon at the top of the table column. Voice messages are sorted by voice message name. An arrowhead pointing up indicates the table is sorted in ascending order. An arrowhead pointing down indicates the table is sorted in descending order.

Setting the Voice Messages Table Preferences

You can set how many rows you want the Call History and Pre-Recorded Voice Messages tables to display on each page. By default, 10 rows are displayed. Click the preferences icon at the bottom, left of the table. A pop-up menu opens. Select the number of rows you want to display on each page from the pop-up menu. The table automatically updates. To set a number of rows that is different than the preset options, select Custom. The Number of Rows to Display dialog box opens. In the text field, type the number of rows you want to display. The number must be between 1 and 1000. Click OK to set the number of rows or Cancel to leave the number of rows unchanged.

Working with Recipient Lists

A recipient list, also called a call list, is a saved group of parents and students who share a common interest, such as the PTA, the school committee, or the activities club. Saved recipient lists can be used to quickly address your email, text, and voice messages. A recipient list can include individuals and specific grade levels, and it can also exclude specific students.

Creating a Call List

Creating a recipient list, or a call list, is easy because the student database is integrated with Parent Messaging. From the Recipient List page, click Add Call List. The New Call List page opens.

First, name the recipient list. This name will be displayed in the "To the Parents of" drop-down menus when addressing email, text, and voice messages. From the Include these Grades, Include these Students, and Exclude these Students text boxes, select the names of the students whose parents you want to include or exclude from the recipient list from the drop-down menus. As you add students and parents, their names and phone numbers are added to the table below. When you are finished, click Save to save the list for use with your QuickSchools communications or click Clear to discard the changes.

Viewing, Editing, and Deleting a Recipient List

To view or edit a recipient list, click the recipient list you want to view in the Call List table. The Call List page displays. Make changes as necessary. Click Save to save the changes, or Clear to leave the list unchanged.

To delete a recipient list you no longer need, simply click Delete at the end of the row.

Searching for a Recipient List

The search feature enables you to search for a specific recipient list by name. Place your cursor in the Search field and type in the word, phrase, or name for which you are searching. Begin your search by pressing Enter on your keyboard or by clicking the magnifying glass icon at the end of the search field. The results of your search display in the Recipient List table. To clear the search results, click Clear Search below the Search field.

Sorting the Recipient List Table

You can sort the Recipient List table in ascending or descending order by clicking the arrow icon at the top of the table column. Recipient Lists are sorted by the date they were created. An arrowhead pointing up indicates the table is sorted in ascending order. An arrowhead pointing down indicates the table is sorted in descending order.

Setting Recipient List Table Preferences

You can set how many rows you want the Recipient List table to display on each page. By default, 10 rows are displayed. Click the preferences icon at the bottom, left of the table. A pop-up menu opens. Select the number of rows you want to display on each page from the pop-up menu. The table automatically updates. To set a number of rows that is different than the preset options, select Custom. The Number of Rows to Display dialog box opens. In the text field, type the number of rows you want to display. The number must be between 1 and 1000. Click OK to set the number of rows or Cancel to leave the number of rows unchanged.
