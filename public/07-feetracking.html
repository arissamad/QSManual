Fee Tracking

The Fee Tracking and Student Billing feature enables you to manage student fees and payments. With Fee Tracking, you can keep an up-to-date record for all fees and charges that students owe. You can make personalized lists of common and standard charges, so adding charges to a student's account is done quickly. For a more streamlined billing system, family billing can be enabled so that all students within a family can be viewed together and charged at the same time.

This chapter offers detailed information on managing charges, generating invoices, recording payments, issuing refunds, making personalized lists of charges, and working with family billing.

Introduction to Fee Tracking

Fee Tracking begins with the Student Billing table. The Student Billing table shows you a summary of students and account balances. Clicking on a student in the Student Billing table enables you to add, view, and delete charges, generate PDF invoices, and issue refunds.

Enabling and Disabling the Fee Tracking Feature

Like all Features in QuickSchools, you can turn on or turn off a feature and configure access rights and privileges via the Features link near the top right of your screen. This ability is limited to the school admin users.

  1. Click Features at the top of the page.

    The Features page opens.

  1. Go to the row that says School Fees Tracking.
  2. To turn on the Fee Tracking feature, check the checkbox in the School Fees Tracking row.

    3. The menu bar refreshes. If the Fee Tracking feature is disabled, then the Fee Tracking option will no longer be available in the top menu bar. If the Fee Tracking feature is enabled, then the Fee Tracking option will be available in the top menu bar.

  3. Click an item from the menu bar to continue. To configure the Fee Tracking feature, see Setting General Fee Tracking Preferences.

Accessing the Fee Tracking Page

To access the Fee Tracking page, click Fee Tracking at the top of the page. If there is no Fee Tracking option at the top of the page, see Enabling and Disabling the Fee Tracking Feature.

The Fee Tracking page opens and the Student Billing table is displayed. The Student Billing table shows a list of students and their outstanding balances.

Moving around the Student Billing Table

By default, 10 students are displayed on each page of the Student Billing table. When more than 10 students are added, a new page in the Student Billing table is added. Use the navigation controls at the bottom of the Student Billing table to scroll through the pages. To change the number of students shown on each page, see Setting Student Billing Table Preferences.

Click on a student name (or anywhere in the row) and you'll see the charges and payment details for the students. You can also change how charges are viewed. Select one of the following views:

Uninvoiced ChargesOnly shows charges that have not been included in an invoice. Any charges that have been included in an invoice will not be shown in this view.
All ChargesShows all invoiced and uninvoiced charges.
Charges by InvoiceShows charges by invoice number and amount.

The invoices in QuickSchools resembles more like a statement of all charges and payments for easy and chronological reference.

Adding a Charge

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to add a charge to. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Click Add Charge.

    The Add Charge dialog box opens.

  1. To change the date of the charge, click the Charge Date field. An interactive calendar opens.

  1. Use the calendar to select the billing date. There are two ways to change the date:
    • Click the arrowheads on either side of the month and year at the top of the calendar. Clicking the left arrow moves the months backwards. Clicking the right arrow moves the months forward. The years move backward or forwards when you reach January.
    • Click the arrow next to the month or year. A drop-down menu opens. Click the month or year you want from the drop-down menu. The month and year display on the calendar.
  2. Click the number of the day that the charge occurred. The calendar closes.

    3. Note: You can only select a date that occurs after your last generated invoice.

  3. In the Category field, click one or more fee categories from the drop-down list. The rest of Add Charge dialog box automatically updates with the category details. You may edit the displayed information if necessary.

    4. Note: If the Category field is not shown, or if you need more information about categories, see Introduction to Fee Categories.

  4. In the Description field, add what the charge is for.
  5. In the Amount field enter how much the charge is for. You do not need to add a currency symbol.

    6. Note: Enter a negative number for refunds. For more information about refunds, see Issuing a Refund.

  6. Click OK to add the charge to the student's account or Cancel to leave the account unchanged.

Deleting a Charge

Charges can be deleted from a student account until an invoice is generated. Once an invoice is generated, charges no longer can be deleted. To reverse a charge after an invoice has been generated, you can cancel the invoice or issue a refund. For more information about invoices and refunds, see Generating Invoices, Canceling Invoices, and Issuing Refunds.

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to add a charge to. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Find the charge you want to delete.
  3. Click Delete at the end of the row to delete the charge. If there is no delete option available, the charge has already been invoiced and can no longer be deleted. To reverse an invoiced charge, you can cancel the invoice or issue a refund. See Canceling Invoices or Issuing Refunds for more information.

Generating an Invoice

You can generate a PDF invoice from a student's account. Invoices in QuickSchools are more closely akin to a credit card statement i.e. both charges and payments are displayed in chronological order for the readers's easy reference. Once the invoice is generated, you can save the file to your computer to be emailed, or you can print a copy to mail. In order to view your PDF invoice, you need to have Adobe Reader installed on your computer. Go to Adobe.com for the latest Adobe Reader software.

When you create an invoice, the invoice includes all the charges, payments, and refunds currently displayed in the Uninvoiced Charges tab.

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to generate an invoice for. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Click Uninvoiced Charges.

  1. Click Generate Invoice for Uninvoiced Charges.

    The Confirm Invoice dialog box displays

  1. Click Yes, Create Invoice. The invoice generates and the Uninvoiced Charges table clears. No charges will be visible in this view until new charges, payments, or refunds are added.

Viewing and Printing an Invoice

In order to view your PDF invoice, you need to have Adobe Reader installed on your computer. Go to Adobe.com for the latest Adobe Reader software.

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to view. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Click Charges by Invoice.

  1. Click Show Invoice Items to see all charges, payments, and refunds on the selected invoice. A list of the charges, payments, and refunds on the invoice displays.

  1. Click Hide Invoice Items to hide the invoice charges.
  2. To view, print, or save a copy of the invoice, click Download PDF. The Opening Invoice dialog box opens.

  1. Click Open to view the invoice in Adobe Reader. From Adobe Reader you can print or save the invoice. Refer to the Adobe Reader documentation for more information about using Adobe Reader.
  2. Click Save File to save a copy of the invoice on your computer.

Canceling an Invoice

You can cancel the last invoice that you have generated. Items from the canceled invoice are returned to the uninvoiced items list and a notation in the Charges by Invoice table shows that the invoice was canceled.

To remove previous charges that have already been invoiced, you can issue a refund. For more information, see Issuing a Refund.

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to view. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Click Charges by Invoice.

  1. Click Cancel Invoice to remove the invoice from the Charges by Invoice table. A confirmation dialog box opens

  1. Click Yes, cancel invoice to cancel the invoice or Cancel to leave the invoice unchanged.

    2. The Charges by Invoice table displays a note indicating that the invoice was canceled and the items from the invoice are moved to the Uninvoiced Charges table.

  2. Click Uninvoiced Charges to view the items in the Uninvoiced Charges table.

Recording a Payment

  1. Click Add Payment. The Add Payment dialog box opens.

  1. To change the date of the payment, click the Payment Date field. An interactive calendar opens.

  1. Use the calendar to select the payment date. There are two ways to change the date:
    • Click the arrowheads on either side of the month and year at the top of the calendar. Clicking the left arrow moves the months backwards. Clicking the right arrow moves the months forward. The years move backward or forwards when you reach January.
    • Click the arrow next to the month or year. A drop-down menu opens. Click the month or year you want from the drop-down menu. The month and year display on the calendar.
  2. Click the number of the day that the payment occurred. The calendar closes.
  3. In the Description field, add what the payment is for.
  4. In the Amount field enter how much the payment is for. You do not need to add a currency symbol.
  5. Click OK to add the payment to the student's account or Cancel to leave the account unchanged. The payment will show as a negative number in the Amount column and be subtracted from the outstanding balance due.

Deleting a Payment

Payments can be deleted from a student account until an invoice is generated. Once an invoice is generated, payments no longer can be deleted. To reverse a payment after an invoice has been generated, you can cancel the invoice or add a new charge. For more information about invoices and adding charges, see Generating Invoices, Canceling Invoices, and Adding Charges.

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to remove a payment from. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Find the payment you want to delete.
  3. Click Delete at the end of the row to remove the payment. If there is no delete option available, the payment has already been invoiced and can no longer be deleted. To reverse an invoiced payment, you can cancel the invoice or add a new charge. See Canceling Invoices or Adding a Charge for more information.

Issuing a Refund

  1. From the Student Billing table, click on the name of the student whose account you want to credit. The student's billing account opens.
  2. Click Add Charge.

    The Add Charge dialog box opens.

  1. To change the date of the refund, click the Charge Date field. An interactive calendar opens.

  1. Use the calendar to select the refund date. There are two ways to change the date:
    • Click the arrowheads on either side of the month and year at the top of the calendar. Clicking the left arrow moves the months backwards. Clicking the right arrow moves the months forward. The years move backward or forwards when you reach January.
    • Click the arrow next to the month or year. A drop-down menu opens. Click the month or year you want from the drop-down menu. The month and year display on the calendar.
  2. Click the number of the day that the refund occurred. The calendar closes.
  3. In the Description field, add what the refund is for.
  4. In the Amount field enter the refund amount as a negative number. For example, if you want to refund $20.00, enter -20.00. You do not need to add a currency symbol.

  1. Click OK to credit the refund to the student's account or Cancel to leave the account unchanged. The payment will show as a negative number in the Amount column and be subtracted from the outstanding balance due.

Sorting Student Billing Accounts

To find information faster, you can sort table columns by ascending or descending order.

  1. Click on the column that you want to sort. The table sorts information by the selected column in ascending order.
  2. Click on the column again to sort by descending order.

An arrowhead displays in the column that the table is being sorted by. An arrowhead pointing up indicates the table is sorted in ascending order. An arrowhead pointing down indicates the table is sorted in descending order.

In this example, the table is sorted by student name in ascending order.

  1. Type in the word or value for which you are searching.
  2. You can begin your search two ways:
    • Press Enter on your keyboard.
    • Click the magnifying glass icon at the end of the search field.

You can set how many rows you want the Student Billing table to display on each page. By default, 10 rows are displayed on each page.

  1. Click the preferences icon at the bottom left of the Student Billing table. A pop-up menu opens.

  1. Select the number of rows you want to display on each page from the pop-up menu. The table automatically updates.
  2. To set a number of rows that is different than the preset options, select Custom. The Number of Rows to Display dialog box opens.
  3. In the text field, type the number of rows you want to display. The number must be between 1 and 1000.

Introduction to Fee Categories

Fee Categories are an easy way for you to manage a charge that is used frequently. Categories are templates that enable you to setup and define a fee one time. The categories you define are available in the Add Charge form and can be applied to a student account in one click. When adding new fees to a student's account, you can select several categories at one time, reducing the time it takes to add multiple fees to students' accounts.

Fee Categories are different from Standard Charges in the way they are applied. Fee Categories are applied to a student through the Add Charge dialog box. On the other hand, students are applied to a Standard Charge enabling you to charge one fee to multiple students or the whole school at the same time. For more information about Standard Charges, see Introduction to Standard Charges.

Enabling and Disabling Fee Categories

By default the Fee Categories feature comes disabled. After enabling the Fee Category feature, the Category Setup menu bar option becomes available on the Student Billing page.

  1. Click Configure. The School Fees Tracking dialog box opens.

  1. Check Enable Each Charge/Payment to be Associated with a Category.

  1. Click the Select Users Who Can Configure Categories text box. A list of faculty members opens.
  2. From the faculty member list, select who can add, define, edit, and delete fee categories.

    3. Note: If you leave this box blank, nobody will be able to create or manage fee categories.

  3. Click Save to save your changes. Click Cancel to leave your preferences unchanged.

Introduction to Standard Charges

Standard charges are an easy way for you to group, manage and apply frequently used fees to large groups of students quickly. A Standard Charge is a collection or set of one or more related fees that share a common student base, such as all 1st grade students. After setting up a Standard Charge, simply open the charge you have created and then add students to the charge. You can add students individually, by homerooms, specific grades, or add the entire school with one click. After applying students to the Standard Charge, you can then customize the charge by individual student, if necessary.

For example, all incoming first graders are required to purchase an art kit which includes crayons, clay, and paper. Instead of using the Student Billing table to find and click on each first grade student in the school and then charging each of those students three times for art supplies, a Standard Charge is created called First Grade Art Kit that includes the three art supplies. After the First Grade Art Kit Standard Charge is defined, simply click on the charge to open it, then click All First Graders to add the charge to all first grade students.

Standard Charges are different from Fee Categories in the way they are applied. Students are applied to a Standard Charge enabling you to charge large groups of students at the same time. Fee Categories are applied to a single student through the Add Charge dialog box. For more information about Fee Categories, see Introduction to Fee Categories.

Introduction to Family Billing

The Family Billing feature is a way to group students from the same family into one account for easy viewing, management, and billing. Student accounts can still billed individually or as a family, and parents are presented with a one invoice that show all of the family charges and fees.

Enabling and Disabling Family Billing

By default the Family Billing feature comes disabled. After enabling the Family Billing feature, the Student Billing table is sorted by family which is determined by primary payer. If no primary payer has been set for your students, the table will be blank. See Setting the Primary Payer for more information.

  1. From the top of the page, click Features.

    The Features page opens.

  1. Go to the row that says School Fees Tracking.

  1. Click Configure. The School Fees Tracking dialog box opens.

  1. Check Turn on Family Billing.

  1. Click Save to save your changes. Click Cancel to leave your preferences unchanged.

Moving around the Family Billing Table

When the Family Billing feature is enabled, students are grouped by primary payer in the Student Billing table. You can view accounts by family or by individual student.

Viewing a Family Account

From the Student Billing table, click the Primary Payer of the family you want to view.

The family account opens showing all students associated with the primary payer and each fee charged to each student.

Viewing an Individual Student Account from a Family Account

From the Student Billing table, click the name of the student whose account you want to view from the Dependents column.

The student’s account opens showing each fee charged to that student.

When viewing a Family Billing account, there are two types of fees displayed in the Description column: individual student fees and fees that include two or more students in a family. Fees that have only been charged to one student will be listed under the description column by the student’s name and a description of the fee. Fees that have been charged to more than one student in the family will be listed as a single entry with the total amount for all charges followed by a link that says Show Items. Click Show Items to display how the charge has been allocated among family members. Click Hide Items to hide the student charges.

For more information about basic Student Billing table navigation and procedures, see Introduction to Fee Tracking and Moving around the Student Billing Table.

Setting the Primary Payer

Setting the primary payer for students is done from the Students page. Students who do not have a primary payer set will not be visible in the Student Billing table when the Family Billing feature is enabled. When using Family Billing, QuickSchools identifies those students who do not have a primary payer. Click Students have no Primary Payer Set link at the top of the Student Billing table to see a list of students who need to have a primary payer set.

  1. To set the primary payer for a student, go to the Students page by clicking Students at the top menu bar.

    The Students page opens.

  1. Click the name of the student who you want to assign a primary payer. The student’s profile opens.

  1. Click the Primary Payer text box to display a drop-down menu of parent names.
  2. From the list, select the name of the parent who is responsible for paying the student’s outstanding charges.
  3. Click Save. The student is now listed in the Student Billing table.

Adding a Student to a Family

To add a student to a family, simply give the new student the same primary payer as the other members of the family. See see Setting the Primary Payer for more information.

Moving or Deleting a Student from a Family Account

It is easy to move a student from one family account to another. All you need to do is change the primary payer. Once the student’s primary payer is changed, the student’s name displays in that payer’s family account. For more information, see Setting the Primary Payer.

To delete or unassign a student from a family account, you need to change the primary payer. You can change the primary payer so that the student displays in a different family account, or use the Not Selected option, which indicates that no parent or guardian has been identified as being responsible for the student’s school fees. If the Not Selected option is used, then the student will not be visible on the Student Billing page.

Tip:To keep students on the Student Billing page even if they don’t have a primary payer, you may want to create an empty or test student account with as many “parents” as you need named “no primary payer,” “scholarship,” “state funded,” or any other appropriate category. You may then assign these categories as a primary payer to students who have special financial arrangements. For more information about adding students, see Adding Student Records Manually.

Adding a Charge to a Family Account

  1. From the Student Billing table, click the Primary Payer of the account you want to charge. The primary payer’s family account opens.
  2. Click Add Charge.

    The Add Family Charge dialog box opens.

  1. To change the date of the charge, click the Charge Date field. An interactive calendar opens.

  1. Use the calendar to select the billing date. There are two ways to change the date:
    • Click the arrowheads on either side of the month and year at the top of the calendar. Clicking the left arrow moves the months backwards. Clicking the right arrow moves the months forward. The years move backward or forwards when you reach January.
    • Click the arrow next to the month or year. A drop-down menu opens. Click the month or year you want from the drop-down menu. The month and year display on the calendar.
  2. Click the number of the day that the charge occurred. The calendar closes.

    3. Note: You can only select a date that occurs after your last generated invoice.

  3. From the Category drop-down menu, select a Fee Category. The Description and Amount fields automatically update with the preset information. You may edit this information if necessary. This field is optional.

    4. Note: For more information about Fee Categories, see Introduction to Fee Categories.

  4. In the Description text box, enter more detail about what the fee is for or edit the default Fee Category information. This field is required.
  5. In the amount text box, enter the total amount being charged to the family. For example, if 4 students in a family are each buying a $10 book, then enter 40.00.
  6. Once you have entered the family total, allocate it among the members of the family in one of these ways:
    7. Allocate EquallyDivides the charge equally among all members of the family. In our book example, each of the 4 students is charged $10.
    Allocate AllApplies the entire charge to a single family member. Use this option to charge one student in a family a fee that applies only to them. For example, the first-grade art kit only needs to be applied to the family’s first grader, not to the third- and sixth-grade students in the family.
    Custom EntryYou can manually enter unequal charges to students in a family. For example, a family might owe $100 in total for their 3 students’ pictures. But first-grade, third-grade, and sixth-grade student packages are different prices. You can charge the first-grader $20, the third-grader $35, and the sixth-grader $45.

    Note: Make sure that the charges you have allocated to the family equal the amount you entered in the Total Amount field. You will not be able to save the charge if the allocation of fees does not equal the Total Amount.

  1. Click Save to save the charges to the family or Cancel to leave the account unchanged.

Deleting a Charge from a Family Account

You can delete a charge from a family account until an invoice is generated for the family. To remove a charge after an invoice has been generated, you will need to issue a refund. For more information about generating invoices and issuing refunds, see Generating an Invoice and Issuing a Refund.

  1. From the Student Billing table, click the Primary Payer of the account you want to edit. The primary payer’s family account opens.
  2. Click Delete next to the charge you want to remove.

  1. A confirmation dialog box displays.
  2. Click Yes, Delete Family Charge to delete the charge, or Cancel to leave the account unchanged.
Refunds

Stripe fees are refunded as well. There are two cases:

  • Partial Refunded: New Stripe fee is calculated as usual on the new charge amount (ie Previous Amount - Refuned Amount). The fee amount refunded is the old fee - new fee amount.
  • Full Refunded: Entire Stripe fee is refunded. (ie 0.00)

QuickSchools fees are never refunded. The original amount is kept.
